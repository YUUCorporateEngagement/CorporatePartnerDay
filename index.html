About Year Up United
Closing the Opportunity Divide — one young adult at a time.
Year Up United is a workforce development organization that connects motivated young adults to the skills, experiences, and professional networks they need to launch high-quality careers. Through an intensive, employer-aligned training model, participants complete technical and professional skills development before moving into paid corporate internships, supported throughout by Year Up United coaches and staff. The result: work-ready, skills-first talent that shows up prepared, motivated, and ready to contribute from day one.
About the Event
A morning built for the future of work.
Corporate Partner Day is Year Up United's signature employer engagement event that brings together corporate leaders, Skills-First talent, and workforce advocates for a morning of connection, insight, and shared purpose.

Whether you're a current partner deepening your commitment or exploring what a skills-first hiring strategy can unlock, this event is designed to make the value concrete, personal, and actionable.

Meet Our Panelists
Featured Panelist
Randy Rodriguez
Systems Development Engineer II — Jamf
Year Up United New York  •  IT Helpdesk Track  •  Class of 2012

Randy's story does not follow a straight line, and that is exactly what makes it worth hearing.

When Randy completed the Year Up United IT Helpdesk program in 2012, he was not just gaining technical skills. He was building a foundation, a professional identity, and a belief in what was possible for his career. More than a decade later, he has grown far beyond where he started, and today serves as a Systems Development Engineer II at Jamf, a global leader in Apple device management, doing work that is complex, meaningful, and in demand.

What Randy will bring to the panel is something no resume can capture: an honest account of what it actually takes to navigate a corporate career, keep growing through uncertainty, and future-proof your professional development in a world that keeps changing. We think you are going to want to hear what he has to say.
